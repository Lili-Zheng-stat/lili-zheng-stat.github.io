Professional Experiences

Lili Zheng

 

I am currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Statistics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.
Before joining the University of Illinois, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Rice University, advised by Prof. Genevera Allen. I completed my Ph.D. in the Department of Statistics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, under the supervision of Prof. Garvesh Raskutti. I received my bachelor's degree at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2016.

Research interest: I am broadly interested in statistical machine learning, where I develop reliable methods and theory for large-scale and complex statistical problems. In particular, I have been working on the following topics: interpretable machine learning, distribution-free inference, multiple testing, ensemble methods, graphical models, missing data, data integration, spectral methods.
